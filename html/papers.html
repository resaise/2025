Call for Papers

The ReSAISE 2025 workshop invites researchers and practitioners to present original research on the reliability and security of AI for Software Engineering (SE). For a full list of relevant topics, please visit the Home Page.

We invite the following types of submissions:

  • Research Papers (up to 8 pages, including references): Original, high-quality research contributions addressing theoretical, empirical, or methodological aspects at the intersection of AI and Software Engineering.
  • Short Papers (up to 4 pages, including references): Preliminary results, focused case studies, or well-defined contributions that are smaller in scope but offer valuable insights.
  • Dataset & Tool Papers (up to 4 pages, including references): Descriptions of datasets, benchmarks, or tools that enable reproducible research and innovation. Submissions must include sufficient detail for reuse, and links to public repositories are strongly encouraged.
  • Position Papers / New Ideas (up to 4 pages, including references): Visionary or thought-provoking ideas, early-stage research, or emerging challenges and opportunities in reliable and secure AI for SE.

Important: Please clearly indicate the type of your submission (e.g., Research Paper, Short Paper, etc.) on the first page of the manuscript.

All papers must be written in English and formatted according to the IEEE authoring guidelines: IEEE Authoring Templates . Please use the same LaTeX/Word templates as the ISSRE Research Track: ISSRE 2025 Submission Info .

Submissions that exceed the page limits, are off-topic, or not formatted correctly will be rejected without review. All accepted papers will be peer-reviewed by members of the program committee.

Accepted papers will be included in the supplemental volume of the ISSRE 2025 proceedings, published by the IEEE Computer Society, and indexed in IEEE Xplore. At least one author of each accepted paper must register for the ISSRE 2025 conference and present the paper at the workshop.
